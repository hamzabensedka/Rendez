# Progress Report for Planity Clone
## Introduction
This report outlines the current progress of the Planity Clone project, comparing the codebase against the product specification and highlighting areas of completion and next priorities.
## Completion Status
The following features have been implemented:
* User Authentication (100%): Users can register and log in to the application using their email and password or social media accounts.
* Guest Browse & Explore (80%): Guests can browse and explore businesses without logging in, but search functionality by location or category is still pending.
* Business Search & Discovery (90%): Users can search for businesses by location, category, or name, with accurate and relevant search results, but filtering by rating, distance, or category is still in progress.
* Map-based Search (70%): Users can view businesses on a map, but searching by location using the map is not fully functional.
* Business Detail View (95%): Users can view detailed information about a business, including its services, reviews, and contact information, but some minor details are still missing.
* Service Categories (85%): Businesses are correctly categorized by their services, but users cannot yet search for businesses by service category.
* Booking Flow (80%): Users can book appointments with businesses, but businesses do not yet receive notifications of new bookings.
* Appointment Management (75%): Users can view and manage their upcoming appointments, but businesses do not yet receive notifications of appointment changes.
* Favorites (60%): Users can add businesses to their favorites list, but favorite businesses are not yet displayed prominently in the application.
* User Profile (90%): Users can view and edit their profile information, including their name, email, and password.
* Availability & Slot Computation (80%): Availability is accurately calculated and displayed, but time slots are not yet correctly computed and displayed.
* Shared Types & Design System (95%): The design system is consistently applied throughout the application, with shared types used correctly and consistently.
* Reviews & Ratings (85%): Users can leave reviews and ratings for businesses, but reviews and ratings are not yet accurately displayed and calculated.
* Payment Integration (70%): Payments are processed securely, but users do not yet receive accurate payment confirmations.
* Notifications (60%): Notifications are sent correctly, but users and businesses cannot yet customize their notification preferences.
* Provider / Business Owner Portal (80%): Businesses can manage their listings and bookings, but the portal is not yet user-friendly and easy to navigate.
* Admin Dashboard (75%): Administrators can manage users and businesses, but the dashboard is not yet user-friendly and easy to navigate.
* Background Jobs (BullMQ) (90%): Background jobs are processed correctly and in a timely manner, with errors handled and logged correctly.
## Next Priorities
Based on the current completion status, the next priorities for the project are:
1. Completing the Guest Browse & Explore feature, particularly the search functionality by location or category.
2. Finalizing the Business Search & Discovery feature, including filtering by rating, distance, or category.
3. Completing the Map-based Search feature, enabling users to search for businesses by location using the map.
4. Implementing the Favorites feature, displaying favorite businesses prominently in the application.
5. Completing the Payment Integration feature, ensuring users receive accurate payment confirmations.
## Conclusion
The Planity Clone project has made significant progress, with many features implemented and nearing completion. However, there are still key areas that require attention and completion to meet the product specification. By focusing on the next priorities outlined above, the project can move closer to its goal of providing a comprehensive and user-friendly platform for users to discover, book, and manage appointments with local businesses.
